# Diet Plan for Heart Diseases

Heart diseases is a broad term comprising of spectrum of diseases associated with disorders of circulation, heart muscle and vessels of the heart. There are many types of heart diseases which affect different areas of the circulatory system. The common causes of heart disease are high cholesterol level, obesity, diabetes, hereditary, smoking, high- fat and high – sodium diet. So, the diet should be low in fat, especially saturated fat and low in sodium in case of Heart problems.

## DIET INSTRUCTIONS

Heart diseases require a carefully planned diet that is low in fat, especially saturated fat, and low in sodium. The diet should focus on light, easy-to-digest foods that do not cause accumulation of endotoxins and help in maintaining strong digestive fire.

Cereals should include wheat, quinoa, buckwheat millets, brown rice, oats, sorghum, corn, finger millet, foxtail millet, little millet, and barley. A recommended flour composition is wheat flour (70%) + barley flour (30%). Refined cereals, white refined flour, pasta, white rice, and freshly harvested rice should be avoided as they cause endotoxin accumulation.

Fruits recommended are apples, cherries, apricot, avocadoes, raspberries, banana, black grapes, peaches, kiwi, squash, pomegranate, mangoes, muskmelons, amla (1-2 in a day), and papaya. Citrus fruits like oranges, raspberries, berries, grapes, lemons, strawberries, kiwi should be avoided as they cause pitta aggravation.

Recommended vegetables include broccoli, sweet potatoes, spinach, asparagus, onions, garlic, ginger, beans, pumpkin, zucchini, peas, carrots, potatoes, snake gourd, bitter gourd, mushroom, bottle gourd, turnips, pumpkin, ash gourd, cauliflower, ginger, tomato, and garlic. Canned vegetables, frozen vegetables, eggplant, and capsicum should be avoided.

For pulses, Yellow Lentils, Green lentils, Soybeans, Horse gram soup, tuvar dal (Pigeon peas), chana dal (Split black gram), whole black gram (limited), and Pink lentils are recommended. Black lentils and kidney beans should be avoided.

Low-fat milk and cow's ghee (limited) are the dairy products to consume. Full fat milk, cheese, yogurt, condensed milk, icecreams, butter, and paneer should be avoided.

For oils, olive oil, cow ghee, and soybean oil are recommended. Palm oil, coconut oil, and hydrogenated oil should be avoided.

Recommended spices include turmeric, fenugreek, cardamom, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, black pepper, dried fenugreek leaves, and coriander leaves. Table salt, red chili powder, green chilli, and excessive use of cooking masala should be avoided.

Coconut water, homemade soups like ash gourd soup or horse gram soup, green tea, soy milk, herbal tea (Arjuna Tea), beetroot juice, pomegranate juice, and amla juice are recommended drinks. Whole milk drinks, caffeine, alcohol, sweetened drinks, carbonated drinks, packaged soup, canned fruit juices, coffee, tea, and soya milk should be avoided.

For flesh foods, egg white, grilled salmon and rohu, and shredded chicken soup are recommended. Red meat, canned and smoked fish, smoked meats like liver, branded meats, glandular meat (brain, liver, kidney), prawns, lobster, crab, lean beef, pork, lamb, ham, rabbit, egg yolk, fish roe, sausages, mutton, salami, bacon, duck, goose, meat pies, chicken skin, and seafood fish should be avoided.

Flax seeds, pumpkin seeds, chia seeds, sunflower seeds, walnuts, almonds, and soaked raisins are recommended dry fruits. Cashew nuts, pistachio, and peanuts should be avoided.

Jaggery is the recommended sweetener. White refined sugar and artificial sweeteners should be avoided.

Homemade foods only are recommended. Bakery products, processed foods, mayonnaise, chutneys, pickles, junk food, deep fried foods, excessive spicy and salty food items, fruit jellies, cream soups, dark chocolate, butterscotch, coconut bar, fiber bars, miso, and too much salty food should be avoided.
