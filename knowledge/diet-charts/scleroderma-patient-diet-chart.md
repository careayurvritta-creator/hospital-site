Scleroderma is a chronic, rare, autoimmune disease in which normal tissues are replaced with thick fibrous tissues. In this condition, our immune system triggers cells to produce too much collagen which results in thickening and fibrosis of the skin. Scleroderma is usually a skin disorder but it also affects the lungs, kidneys, muscles, and joints. A balanced diet is very beneficial for scleroderma because people with scleroderma experience symptoms like poor appetite, weight loss, and malnutrition. This balanced diet helps to maintain the ideal weight and avoid the risk of heart, lung, and kidney problems. Here, we need to avoid foods that aggravate symptoms such as citrus fruit, greasy fried food, spicy food, carbonated beverages, and alcohol.

HOME REMEDIES

TURMERIC – Take 1 tsp of turmeric powder and mix it with water and make a paste, Apply this paste to affected areas.

SPINACH – Add Spinach to your daily diet in the form of Soup or Mild Salads.

FISH OIL – It can be applied on patches. Fish oil reduces inflammation.

PEPPERMINT OIL – Mix 5-6 drops of peppermint oil with coconut oil properly and apply it on the affected area.

ALOE VERA – Applying Fresh Aloe Vera Helps in Relieving Burning – if present.

OTHER TIPS / RECOMMENDATIONS FOR SCLERODERMA PATIENTS

Do not skip any meal, especially Breakfast.
Eat small meals frequently rather than large meals.
Increase Consumption of seasonal fruits and vegetables.
Must Drink 8- 10 Glasses of Water Daily, it detoxifies.
Walking for 30 min is Recommended.
Whole Fruit should be Preferred to Fruit Juices.
Avoid Smoking and Alcohol.
Food items not mentioned in this diet chart can be consumed in moderation.
In General, all kinds of artificial, packed, industrial, and processed food items should be avoided.
Behavior plays an important role in preventing diseases, so keep yourself away from negative thoughts, anger, greed, and Ego.
